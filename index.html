Project Details

Scene Description

Most B2C lead generation workflows suffer from high friction and data fragmentation, where scraped competitor reviews sit in static files rather than being prioritized by intent or managed in a live sales pipeline.

Current State

Using first principles, we find public Google Maps reviews to identify active customers and utilize a Python-based scoring engine to benchmark lead quality based on budget, urgency, and service type.

Technique

Developed a modular lead-management framework, beginning with a Python bridge to generate targeted social search URLs, implementing a scoring algorithm with keyword-weighting, and incorporating a live-sync CRUD interface with Airtable for automated lead persistence.

Future Scope

The next stage of development involves implementing automated sentiment analysis on review text to further refine "Hot Lead" detection and integrating a direct LinkedIn/Instagram messaging API to reduce outreach latency.

Development of Kinetic Electron Model

Hybrid Subcylcing Motion of Ion and electron - Sample

Project Details

Scene Description

Many Particle-In-Cell(PIC) codes, relies magnetic flux coordinates to model the equilibrium magnetic field, which however faces a singularity at the X-point.

Current State

Using first principles, based on cylindrical coordinates we model the plasma equilibrium and perform the PIC simulation benchmarking the ITG Turbulent transport.

Technique

Developed a kinetic electron model, beginning with state definitions, implementing electron sub-cycling pushes within a hybrid framework, and incorporating the gyrokinetic Poisson equation.

Future Scope

The next stage of development involves benchmarking ITG and TEM turbulence within the kinetic electron model.

NBI Effects in Stellarator (W7-X)

Magnetic Flux Surface of a Stellarator

Project Details

Scene Description

The stabilization of turbulent transport through neoclassical effects induced by NBI heating has been studied for a long time; however, realistic modeling of these effects remains unavailable even today.

Available Methods

Codes for modeling NBI and its neoclassical effects have been studied in various ways in tokamak scenarios, and to a limited extent in stellarator plasmas.

Techniques

A complete modeling of NBI particles in stellarator plasmas was carried out using realistic data and first-principle methods implemented in FORTRAN.

Results and Future Scope

The reproduction of the NBI energy distribution was achieved in its thermalized state. The effects of NBI on neoclassical and turbulent transport can be further extended for detailed study.
