About Me

Joydeep Das is a PhD Scholar at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, specializing in Computational Physics with expertise in High-Performance Computing (HPC). With over four years of experience in developing massively parallel codes for GPU and CPU architectures, the focus is on advancing Particle-in-Cell (PIC) simulations and implementing neural network-assisted particle tracking.

Proficiency in working with world-class supercomputing systems like Perlmutter (NERSC, USA) and PARAM Pravega (IISc, India) has been achieved, with skills in optimizing parallel code performance, configuring computing environments using SPACK, and managing job schedulers such as PBS and SLURM. Extensive experience includes porting codes from CPU to GPU, as well as compiling, debugging, and executing code across various HPC platforms.

Publications and research projects span a wide range of fields, including fusion energy simulations, polymer dynamics, and astrophysical spacetime studies. Demonstrated capabilities in applying machine learning, physics simulations, and software development to solve complex computational challenges.

Dedicated to making a positive societal impact through technology and sustainable practices.
