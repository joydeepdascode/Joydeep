Molecular Dynamics Simulation of a coarse-grained linear polymer chain model under varying conditions; and estimation of the Œ∏-solvent of the model

GitHub: Link

Introduction

Polymers are collections of monomers, each of which is composed of atoms and molecules. Polymers in solvents exhibit complex properties, one of which behaves like a 'model chain', such as how a real gas can behave like an ideal gas under certain conditions This model chain behavior of polymer solutions in this can explore interesting properties such as solubility in polymer-solvent systems The use of simulations of kinematics.

Problem Statement

The project aims to simulate a coarse-grained linear polymer model in an implicit solvent to analyze the behavior of the mean-squared radius of gyration, Rg2, under varying conditions of temperature and viscous forces. By systematically adjusting these parameters, the simulation seeks to identify the polymer system's Œ∏-point. The approach leverages the fact that in a coarse-grained polymer model with N beads per chain, plotting Rg2 against temperature or damping coefficient for different N values will yield curves that intersect at the Œ∏-point. This point represents the polymer's critical solubility behavior, providing insight into the thermodynamic properties of polymer solutions in various solvents.

Techniques and Tools Used

  • Simulation Tool: LAMMPS (Large-scale Atomic/Molecular Massively Parallel Simulator)

  • Model Used: Coarse-grained linear model of polymer chains (bead-spring model)

  • Interaction Potentials:

    • FENE potential (Finite extensible nonlinear elastic) for bonded interactions

    • Lennard-Jones potential for non-bonded interactions

  • Unit System: Reduced units (dimensionless quantities)

  • Configuration Generation: Python script for initial simulation setup

  • Coordinate System: (y,z) coordinates for polymer chain positioning, parallel to the x-axis
